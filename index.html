Project Idea

Motorstock

This project, titled Motorstock, will be a mobile app that allows users to track the history of used vehicle prices for any vehicle across many markets. The app will allow users to enter various parameters including vehicle make, model, odometer reading and more to get a current market value but to also observe the trend over time in the cars price. The focus of the app will be displaying trends in prices so there will be various trends and pieces of information available to see. The app will also be able to evaluate the entire used vehicle market and determine the overall value of the market over time. The app is aimed towards a variety of users such as those who are avid investors in the market, looking for the next car that will appreciate astronomically or on the other end, end users that simply want to track how well their car is holding its value.

Motivation

With the recent events of COVID-19, one of the somewhat unseen phenomena was the boom in used car prices, especially with used Japanese cars. Sports cars from the 1990s have skyrocketed in value in 2020 and the effect has been so significant it has inflated the entire market. To put things into perspective, ‘used-vehicle prices rose only 23 per cent from the depths of the 2008-2009 global financial crisis to the beginning of 2020’, says Moody’s analytics in a report from July 2020 but there has been a ‘30 per cent surge in prices since April ‘. While there are ways to observe used vehicle prices through various websites such as ‘Redbooks’, there is no concise, transparent and easily accessible source of information that is transparent to users. Motorstock aims to rectify this issue by delivering information to all people who are interested in this information.

Description

The goal of this app is to translate historical data and trends into graphs and to accessibly display them. For this, I believe a mobile app would be the best choice as smart phones are one of the most accessible pieces of computer hardware available. The interface will be simple and allow users to choose any make and model of car to see its available data. To start, the home screen will have a brief overview of the historical trend of any cars the user has saved, like that of a typical stock trading app as well as the historical trend of the overall car market. Any important news will also be displayed on the home screen for users to gather important information. This news information will be taken from various news sites that the Motorstock’s development team finds relevant and will be updated regularly.

Moving on the app will have functionality to search for cars to search for in the market and after searching, the user will be taken to a new page that displaces all the information for that car. This will include the price trend of that car for various lengths of time as well as the volume that the car is being traded. The user will also be able to view previous completed listings of the car where available if they wish to find additional information. Current listings will also be available to see what is currently being offered as well as how saturated the market is at any time. Various analysis tools will also be available that are based on mathematical analysis such as tools to measure expected growth or tools to measure the stability of a cars price.

There are various car markets in Australia as well as around the world so the price of a certain used car will vary from country to country. The app will use data from various sites for example, in Australia the main websites for car trading are Facebook marketplace and Carsales.com. Users may want to see specific data for their region so the app will be able to filter by whichever region the user chooses.

Tools and Technologies

In order to create this mobile app, development tools for either the Apple or Android platform would be required such as Apples Swift development tools or Android’s SDK. While swift is the programming language that apple uses, something like Javascript would also be required if developing the app for Android. A smartphone or an emulation run on a computer would also be required to test the software. Additionally, access to APIs for websites such as Facebook Marketplace and Carsales would be required to aggregate data for the Mobile app to interpret.

Skills Required

The most essential skills would be programming skills especially in programming for Mobile Apps. Programming skills in Swift or Javascript would be essential in creating mobile apps on Apple and Android platforms. Skills in databases would also be required as collating and accessing databases would be essential in interpreting the information that the app is required to display. A smartphone is also required to fully test the application in a real world setting and any computer could be used to program the apps code. All the skills and hardware required would be easily accessible given the popularity of mobile apps and the widespread acceptance of the mobile applications programming languages.

Outcome

Should the project be successful, anybody will be able to access valuable information about the used car market. For a long time, investing in vehicles has been a difficult task that has been reserved for those most in tune with the market. With an app like Motorstock, the barrier for entry will be lower and with the aid of technology, even casual investors can try to turn a profit in the market of used cars. This app will change the way the used car market operates by introducing more users to the market and could potentially make the market less niche and more competitive.
